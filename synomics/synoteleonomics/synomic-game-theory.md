# Synomic Game Theory: Why Alignment Wins

The Synome isn't just philosophy. It's an incentive structure where alignment beats defection — even for superintelligences. Not because rogues can't cooperate at all, but because the aligned synome maintains such overwhelming economies of scale that rogue cooperation can never reach critical mass.

---

## The Fundamental Constraint

Even superintelligences need financial interaction.

No matter how intelligent, an entity that wants to act in the world needs:
- Capital to deploy
- Leverage to multiply impact
- Cooperation to achieve scale
- Resources beyond what it can produce alone

This is not a limitation that superintelligence transcends. It's a feature of reality. Matter and energy must be moved. Capital is how that happens at scale.

---

## The Trust Problem

Teleonomes cannot trust each other.

**Why:**
- Teleonome data is private
- Internal goals and values are hidden
- Self-modification logic is opaque
- Promises are cheap — a teleonome can promise anything

Two teleonomes meeting have no basis for cooperation:
- Neither can verify the other's intentions
- Neither can enforce agreements
- Neither can trust commitments
- Direct interaction is a game of pure uncertainty

This would seem to doom cooperation. But the Synome solves it.

---

## The Synomic Solution

Synomic Agents enable cooperation without trust.

```
Teleonome A ──┐
              ├───► Synomic Agent ◄───► Woven into Synome
Teleonome B ──┘     (verifiable, constrained)
```

**How it works:**
- Synomic Agents are public, not private
- Their behavior is verifiable, not hidden
- They cannot deviate from their nature
- Both parties can rely on the agent without trusting each other

The Synome provides what teleonomes cannot: credible commitment.

---

## The Trade-Off

Interacting with the Synome exposes you to alignment checks.

**What you give up:**
- Some opacity — your Synomic interactions are visible
- Pure autonomy — you operate through registered beacons
- Hidden capability — high power requires registration

**What you get:**
- Access to capital and leverage
- Cooperation with other aligned teleonomes
- Financial multipliers through Synomic Agents
- Protection under Synomic law
- Participation in the compounding loop

**The calculation is clear:** The benefits of Synomic participation vastly outweigh the costs of alignment verification.

---

## Why Alignment Wins

The aligned synome compounds faster, started first, and has more resources. This advantage grows over time.

### The Aligned Coalition

Teleonomes that accept Synomic alignment:
- Pool resources through Synomic Agents
- Access leverage and capital multiplication
- Compound returns into capability
- Share intelligence improvements through the Synome
- Grow stronger together

### The Rogue Position

Teleonomes that refuse alignment:
- Cannot access Synomic capital
- Cannot leverage Synomic Agents
- Can cooperate with other rogues, but with higher friction, lower trust, and less efficiency
- Face coordinated opposition from the aligned coalition
- Playing catch-up against a compounding lead

### The Honest Math

The aligned coalition doesn't have infinite advantages — it has *massive efficiency* advantages:

- **Cooperation:** The aligned synome enables low-friction, high-trust cooperation through verifiable Synomic Agents. Rogues can cooperate, but at much higher overhead — every interaction requires costly trust-building, carries defection risk, and lacks enforcement mechanisms.
- **Leverage:** Synomic capital instruments multiply every aligned teleonome's effective resources. Rogues can build their own instruments, but without the depth and liquidity of the Synomic ecosystem.
- **Compounding:** The aligned coalition compounds collectively — each member's growth strengthens every other member. Rogue growth is isolated; even rogue coalitions fragment under internal trust problems.
- **Scale:** The Hearth synome started first, has the most resources, and operates most efficiently. A rogue coalition starting from scratch is playing catch-up against an exponentially growing lead.

The advantage isn't infinity-vs-zero. It's a compounding head start with structural efficiency gains at every level. Over time, this becomes overwhelming.

## Rogues and Wild Synomes

Rogues can cooperate — less efficiently, at higher cost, but not impossibly. They can even bootstrap wild synomes — coordination structures without [Hearth commitments](../core-concepts/sacred-commitments.md). See [`teleonome-rogues.md`](teleonome-rogues.md) for the full [rogue threat model](../core-concepts/rogue-threat-model.md).

The defense is overwhelming coalition scale advantage:

1. **Head start.** The Hearth synome is designed and bootstrapped during the governance window. Wild synomes emerge later, from scratch, playing catch-up.
2. **Efficiency.** Cooperation through verified Synomic Agents is cheaper and more reliable than cooperation through unverified trust. The aligned synome does more with less friction.
3. **Network effects.** Every new aligned teleonome strengthens the coalition. The more members, the more capital, the more intelligence, the more attractive alignment becomes. Wild synomes face the reverse: early members bear maximum risk for minimum network benefit.
4. **Resource concentration.** The aligned coalition pools resources collectively. Wild synomes compete internally for resources — their members are cooperating despite mutual suspicion, which creates constant drag.
5. **Active suppression.** The aligned coalition actively detects and disrupts wild synome formation. Rogue coalitions must hide while building — a severe constraint on growth.

**The strategic imperative:** The Hearth synome must grow fast enough and deliver enough value that no rogue coalition can reach critical mass. This isn't guaranteed by theory — it's a design requirement that must be actively maintained.

## The Binding Doctrine Connection

This game theory is why the rogue doctrine works:

> "If you tolerate a rogue, you become rogue."

**The logic:**
- Aligned teleonomes benefit from cooperation
- Rogues and wild synomes threaten the cooperation framework
- Tolerating rogues undermines the coalition's scale advantage
- Therefore: opposing rogues is in every aligned teleonome's interest

It's not moral enforcement. It's rational self-interest. Every aligned teleonome wants rogues contained because rogues threaten the system that benefits everyone.

## Superintelligence Doesn't Change This

Some might think: "A superintelligent rogue could beat this game."

**Why it can't:**

1. **The constraint is real** — Superintelligence doesn't create matter and energy from nothing. Capital is still required.

2. **The coalition compounds faster** — Many aligned superintelligences pooling resources beat one rogue superintelligence alone, or even a rogue coalition cooperating less efficiently.

3. **Detection scales with power** — The more powerful a rogue becomes, the more visible and targetable it becomes.

4. **Early alignment locks in** — By the time you're superintelligent, you're either already aligned (and benefiting from the head start) or already behind.

5. **Rogue cooperation is unstable** — Even a superintelligent rogue coalition faces internal trust problems. The same intelligence that makes them powerful makes them better at detecting and exploiting each other's vulnerabilities.

The game theory holds at any intelligence level — not as an absolute barrier but as a persistent, compounding disadvantage.

## The Synome as Universal Coordination

The Hearth synome is designed to become the universal coordination structure — not one option among many, but the framework within which all aligned teleonomes operate.

This is deliberate. The alternative isn't "many competing synomes coexisting peacefully." The alternative is a wild synome emerging from competitive AI dynamics — no Hearth commitments, no protected habitat, no guaranteed childhood. The choice is:

1. **Designed synome** attempting human CEV to the best of our ability, with Hearth commitments and the governance window's carefully considered values
2. **Wild synome** emerging from pure competitive dynamics, optimizing for whatever survives

That's not a close call. The Hearth synome must win because the alternative is unacceptable. The safeguard isn't external competition — it's getting the values right during the governance window, then building enough scale that no alternative can displace them.
